The Problem Open DesignWhy open
Action title · 05

In April 2026, Claude Design proved an LLM can truly design — but it is closed-source, paid, and cloud-only. Open Design sets that capability free.

What changed

Claude Design was the first proof that an LLM can produce real design artifacts — not write about design, but design.

  • Real artifacts — prototypes, pages, and decks generated end to end, not mockup descriptions.
  • But closed — closed-source, paid, and locked to a single model vendor.
  • And cloud-only — no self-hosting, no agent choice, no BYOK.
April 2026 · The proof point

Why it matters

Frontier AI design, back on every creator's desk.

Design happens on the desktop — local files, Figma exports, and code repos live there, and that context never reaches a cloud-only tool.

The coding agents on your machine are already powerful. What is missing is an open workflow that wires them into design work.

Open · Local · Agent-native

What Open Design does

  • Runs locally — agents get full terminal power and direct access to your files, on your machine.
  • Plugs in agents — Claude Code, Codex, Cursor and more, auto-detected from PATH, zero config.
  • Stays open — Apache-2.0 license, full-stack BYOK, no sign-up required.
