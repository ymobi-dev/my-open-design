Deep Dive 10

Why design belongs on the desktop, not the cloud

Desktop-Native

Design happens where the work lives. Local files, Figma exports, and code repositories are directly readable — no uploads, no sync, no copies of your context drifting in someone else's cloud.

Agents get full terminal power: they run builds, diff artifacts, and write real files into your project, the same way an engineer on your team would.

The implication is simple: the design artifact is a file you own, version, and ship — not a session you rent.

Agents, Not Rebuilt

Open Design does not build yet another agent. The coding agents already on your machine — Claude Code, Codex, Cursor — are wired into a complete design workflow.

Every choice you make compounds: design systems, preferences, and memory accumulate, so the next generation lands closer to what you wanted.

The takeaway: the platform is the workflow and the memory, while the intelligence stays swappable — yours to choose, via BYOK.
