Open Design · Annual LetterI · Why Open

Dear creators,

In April 2026, Claude Design proved for the first time that an LLM can truly design — not write about design, but produce the design itself. Yet it is closed-source, paid, and cloud-only: the model is locked in, and switching agents, self-hosting, and BYOK are all off the table. So let us open this letter with a single sentence: Open Design exists to bring frontier AI design capability back to every creator's desk.

Open, local, agent-driven — we put the entire journey from idea to prototypes, pages, slides, and HTML video back on your own machine. That choice earned us the two numbers we treasure most.

II · Three Commitments

We don't build agents. We plug them in.

The first is about the desktop. Design happens on the desktop — local files, Figma exports, and code repositories are readable in place, and the agent has the full power of your terminal. The second is about agents: the Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor already on your machine are strong enough. We don't rebuild them — we wire them into a complete design workflow.

The third is the hardest: it learns you over time. Every choice settles into design systems, preferences, and memory, so the next generation lands closer to what you meant — a tool shouldn't have to meet you from scratch every time.

IV · Workflow & the Year Ahead

From idea to delivery in 5 steps

Inside Open Design, an idea becomes a deliverable in five steps: choose a starting point — describe the goal in one sentence, or begin from a template or plugin; set the visual direction — palettes, type, and spacing flow straight into generation; let the agent read the full context and generate a real, runnable artifact in a sandbox; hand it off to engineering, or turn it into a marketing video with HyperFrames; and finally let memory settle — every choice becomes context for the next generation, more precise with every use.

There are too many people to thank. Thank you to the 300+ contributors who left code, plugins, and design systems in the repository — and to every creator who voted with a star, an issue, or a PR. In the year ahead we will do exactly one thing: bring frontier AI design capability back to every creator's desk.
